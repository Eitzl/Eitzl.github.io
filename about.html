Hi, I'm Evan Fitzhugh — an undergraduate at UC Santa Cruz pursuing a double major in Biotechnology and History. I'm interested in how computational tools can be applied to problems in the natural sciences, from genomics to atmospheric prediction, and in the broader historical and political forces that shape how science and society evolve together.

Outside of coursework I spend time on programming projects, reading history and political theory, and occasionally writing about both. When I'm not at a desk you'll find me climbing, hiking, backpacking, mountain biking, or overlanding.

Background

I'm originally from the Bay Area — born in Oakland, raised in Berkeley, and currently living in Santa Cruz.

Interests

Bioinformatics, computational biology, genomics, machine learning applications in biology, history of science, political economy of technology, science policy, science communication, history of redress, history of war crime, history of activism, climbing, hiking, backpacking, mountain biking, overlanding.
